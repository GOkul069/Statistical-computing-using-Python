High-Margin Growth Strategy- Identifying Product Combinations That Drive Spend

Introduction

To identify potential cross-selling opportunities, we analyzed the different types of product categories purchased by customers at REGORK. Our focus was on distinguishing between high-margin and high-volume products, with the aim of combining these categories strategically. By promoting complementary product categories together, we sought to uncover growth potential and opportunities to increase overall sales and profitability.

Based on this objective, we formulated two central research questions:

  • Which high-margin product categories are most frequently purchased together with other categories, indicating strong cross-selling affinity?

  • Which product bundles could REGORK test to increase average basket value and margin among target customers?

To answer these questions, we used the Complete Journey dataset, focusing primarily on the transactions, products, and demographics tables to derive meaningful insights into customer purchasing behavior.

By the end of this analysis, REGORK will gain a clearer understanding of the relationships between product categories and receive data-driven recommendations to support sales growth and margin improvement.

In[17]:
from collections import Counter

# Group categories per basket
basket_groups = tx.groupby("basket_id")["product_category"].apply(list)

pair_counter = Counter()

for cats in basket_groups:
    # remove None values
    cats = [c for c in cats if pd.notna(c)]
    
    # ensure uniques
    unique_cats = list(set(cats))
    
    # skip empty or single-item baskets
    if len(unique_cats) < 2:
        continue
    
    # count all pairs
    for i in range(len(unique_cats)):
        for j in range(i+1, len(unique_cats)):
            # no sorting needed, but if you want consistent ordering:
            pair = tuple(sorted([unique_cats[i], unique_cats[j]]))
            pair_counter[pair] += 1

# top 15 pairs
top_pairs = pair_counter.most_common(15)

Conclusion and Final Recommendations

We aimed to identify product bundles within the Complete Journey dataset that represent customer purchasing behavior at REGORK and have the potential to increase sales and drive growth. Our analysis shows that while high-margin categories contribute relatively modest total sales compared to core grocery staples, they present a strong opportunity for profitable growth. Customers consistently pair these high-margin items such as prepaid wireless products, smoking cessation items, party trays with high-traffic essentials like fuel, soft drinks, milk, bread, and snacks. Additionally, basket composition patterns reveal that most shoppers make focused, low-category trips, but a significant number engage in larger multi-category purchases, creating multiple avenues for cross-sell strategies. The consistent co-occurrence between high-margin and essential categories indicates clear potential to leverage natural shopping behavior to increase both basket size and overall profitability. For a more detailed and customer-oriented approach, it would be helpful to analyze which customers purchase which essential product categories and how frequently they do so, in order to offer high-margin products specifically targeted to them. However, this goes beyond the scope of our current analysis and would require a separate, more in-depth analysis.
